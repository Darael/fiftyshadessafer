BDSM links and community

Nothing’s better for research than lived experience! It can be a bit daunting going into kink spaces, and if you don’t want to, nobody will judge you, but most kinksters are friendly and welcoming - if you aren’t ready for a fetish event where people will be doing BDSM things, a lot of local communities hold ‘munches’, where you can go and meet other kinksters in a casual environment. Here are some places to get info on these things.

Fetlife
Fetlife is probably the kink scene’s most popular website - it’s a social networking site for kinksters
London Fetish Fair
One of the biggest UK fetish meetups, so it’s a great place to get advice, meet people and buy equipment
Brum Bazaar
Birmingham’s fetish fair

Where to buy sex toys and other equipment

Buying good toys to bring into your play is really important, both for your enjoyment and for your safety. Cheap toys can be made of unsafe or carcinogenic(!) materials or be poorly made so they promote infection. This is one area where it really is worth forking out a bit more!

LoveHoney
Bondara
Despite what the name might imply, Bondara are a general rather than a kink-specific online sex shop
Trussed UK
A good range of BDSM-specific toys
Edgeplay
A bit more high-end both in quality and price
Oh Joy Sex Toy
When we first put this up, we called it "A fun comic that’s also a fantastic introduction to sex toy safety, care and recommendations". There have been some high-profile... problems... since then, but overall they're still pretty good. OJST's advice on what's necessary for safety ("don't do this thing", "make sure you do that") is usually solid, but occasionally in the other direction there's a recommendation that isn't safe. Take with a pinch of salt, as with most sources of advice on these matters.
