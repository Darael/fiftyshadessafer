Why 50 Shades Safer?

50 Shades Safer exists because many people in the kink community have been expressing concerns over unhealthy or dangerous trends of people taking their BDSM cues directly from 50 Shades of Grey. While it’s great that people are gaining an interest in kink, most newcomers to kink lack the experience to identify behaviours that, while they may make for sexy fantasies and appear to be reasonable, are actually very dangerous or poor practise (like jumping straight into a 24/7 Master/slave dynamic from a point of no experience, or using cable ties or silk ties for bondage). This isn’t to condemn 50 Shades of Grey, only to say that it can be difficult to access accurate and grounded information on safe kink if you don’t know where to look. And so 50 Shades Safer was born, as a way to make kink information and advice more accessible to newcomers.

How can I get in touch?

If you’ve got questions, comments, advice, submissions, or you just want to chat, you can email us at 50shadessafer@gmail.com and we’ll see what we can do to help you out. You can also post general stuff related to the project to the #50shadessafer tag on Tumblr or Twitter. We don’t currently have a fetlife account, but if anyone wants to set one up that would be awesome.

How can I get involved?

50 Shades Safer is very much a community project, and we welcome submissions, resources and advice from experienced kinksters. Unfortunately, we can’t guarantee that your ideas will show up on the site, and for space reasons we can’t credit everyone who helps us out, but we really want to make this as much of a communally-led thing as possible.

If you’d like to help us spread awareness, you have our blanket approval to pass this website on to anyone who might find it helpful. In the wake of the opening of the 50 Shades of Grey movie, we’re also running a poster campaign to promote the values of safe kink - if you’d like to join in, a printable version of the poster can be downloaded here. All we ask is that you try and refrain from doing anything illegal with them.

If there’s anything practical you think you could help improve on, please let us know - the team who built this website did it under some time pressure, and if you think you can help us make it better, we’d love your help. If you want to help in this way, you can either contact us in the ways mentioned above, or if you've got a bit of technical knowhow the entire site's code is on GitHub, where we would love to receive pull requests.
