SAFE: Keeping injury out of the equation

BDSM done properly can be fun, exciting and safe. But like anything else, there are risks, and kink gone wrong can lead to injury, trauma or even (very occasionally) put your life in danger, so it’s important to be risk-aware and do everything you can to make sure your kinky fun stays fun.

Psychological health and aftercare

Avoiding trauma is as important as avoiding injury!

  • Always, always, always make sure everyone knows what they’ re getting into. What can be fun for some people can be horrifying for others, so before you try something out, make sure you’ve researched it and know exactly what it entails.
  • Discuss everything you want to do with your partner BEFOREHAND. This means having an actual, free conversation, in which you discuss what about it works for each of you and what, if anything, to avoid. It notably does not mean plonking a contract down in front of someone. That's not how you conversation, whatever a certain bestselling book series might have to say.
  • If someone doesn’t explicitly say they want something, then don’t do it. It can be tempting to surprise someone with something unexpected and exciting, but it’s only ok to do that if you’ve already discussed whether or not they’d like it - both the act and the being surprised. If you aren’t sure, just ask.

Talk about what was fun, what wasn’t, what it made you think you might like… in fact, this is a good idea for non-kinky (or "vanilla") sex, too!

BDSM scenes can be pretty intense, especially for the submissive, so the Dom should always check in with the sub - make sure they’re ok, see if they need to talk about anything, if they need to cry, if they need a cup of tea and a hug.   • It’s often nice to do something relaxing and comfortable after a BDSM scene - curling up on the sofa, watching a movie together, sticking on some crap TV and having a glass of wine, it’s all good. Kink and BDSM is all about excitement and tension, and it’s a good idea to wind down from that and take some time to recover.
