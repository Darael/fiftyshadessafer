HELLO!

The global phenomenon that is 50 Shades of Grey has got people trying out kink and BDSM in amazing numbers, and we think that’s great! Experimenting with new things and exploring your sexuality is fantastic, and the more people have their options opened up, the better! But BDSM can be both daunting and dangerous, and while 50 Shades has made a good start in introducing the concepts, it’s important to remember that it’s a fantasy, and it doesn’t cover all the bases when it comes to starting out in BDSM safely.

50 Shades Safer is a community project set up to help newcomers to BDSM bridge the gap between fantasy and reality. Whether you want to fling yourself no-holds-barred into the kink scene, or just try out a bit of bondage in the bedroom, we’re here to help you do it in a way that’s safe, fun and good for you!

For the basic facts on safety in BDSM, we’ve set up some brief rundowns, broken down to match the three parts of one of the major mantras of the kink community: "Safe, Sane, and Consensual". Safe BDSM Practice will take you through some need-to-knows to avoid injury while you play, Danger Signs explains how to tell the difference between a healthy, safe BDSM relationship and an abusive one, and Consent explains how to make sure everyone’s involved is ok with what’s happening throughout. We’re going to add a glossary soon, to help you through kink-specific jargon and deal with some misconceptions, and there’s already a Resources page so you can do some serious research later.

Whether you try some of this out and decide it’s not for you, or get hooked, make sure you do your research, take care of yourself and your partner, and have fun! We’re rooting for you!
